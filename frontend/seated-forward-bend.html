Deepen your stretch and calm your mind with this classic yoga pose.

How to Perform

Begin seated in Staff Pose (Dandasana) with your legs extended straight in front of you and your spine upright. Inhale and raise your arms overhead, lengthening through the spine. As you exhale, hinge at the hips to fold forward, keeping the spine long. Reach your hands toward your feet, ankles, or shins, depending on your flexibility. Hold the pose for 30 seconds to 1 minute, breathing deeply.

Benefits

  • Stretches the spine, shoulders, and hamstrings
  • Stimulates the liver, kidneys, ovaries, and uterus
  • Improves digestion and relieves constipation
  • Calms the brain and helps relieve stress and mild depression
  • Soothes headache and anxiety and reduces fatigue

Tips for Best Results

  • Use a strap around your feet if you can't reach them comfortably
  • Keep your spine long; avoid rounding the back excessively
  • Engage your thigh muscles to deepen the stretch safely
  • Practice after warming up to enhance flexibility
