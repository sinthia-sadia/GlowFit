🐶 Downward Dog

A powerful yoga pose to stretch, strengthen, and rejuvenate your entire body.

How to Do Downward Dog

Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana) is a fundamental yoga pose that stretches the whole body while improving circulation and calming the mind.

  1. Begin on all fours in a tabletop position.
  2. Spread your fingers wide and press your palms into the mat.
  3. Lift your hips upward, straightening your legs and forming an inverted "V".
  4. Keep your head between your arms and heels reaching toward the floor.
  5. Hold the position for several deep breaths.

🏋️ Equipment Needed

  • Yoga mat for support
  • Comfortable clothing

💪 Benefits

  • Stretches hamstrings, calves, and spine
  • Strengthens arms, shoulders, and legs
  • Improves posture and blood flow
  • Relieves stress and calms the mind

✅ Tips

  • Keep a slight bend in your knees if your hamstrings are tight
  • Focus on lengthening the spine rather than touching the heels to the ground
  • Engage your core to stabilize the pose
  • Take slow, deep breaths throughout

🔥 Estimated Calories Burned

Roughly 30–50 calories per 10 minutes depending on intensity and body weight.
